The Lubavitch Youth Organization was established by the seventh Chabad-Lubavitch Rebbe, Rabbi Menachem Mendel Schneerson zy"a, to provide services and educational programs for Jewish youth and adults of all ages and backgrounds.

At a 1955 gathering, the Rebbe declared that "one should focus on tangible actions" (peulas m'mashi'os). This philosophy became the foundation of LYO's mission.

The establishment of LYO was part of the Rebbe's broader vision to invigorate Jewish life globally through extensive outreach and social services, building on the foundations laid by his father-in-law, the sixth Rebbe.

The core philosophy of Chabad is to reach every single Jew with unconditional love and acceptance, regardless of their background or affiliation, and to connect them with their heritage.

LYO translates this philosophy into action by offering numerous programs in the New York City Metro area, including Long Island and Westchester. Each Chabad House offers its own unique combination of programs.

"Today's LYO represents an important part of the vast network of over 3,500 Chabad institutions across the globe."
